Changes And Updates On The Website

Building a website is no easy task, and as a passionate web developer, I recently underwent the exhilarating experience of creating my own online platform. However, the marks and feedback I received for my website left me with some valuable lessons. In this blog post, I would like to share my journey and the insightful feedback I received regarding three crucial aspects: semantic markups, headers, and image performance.
Semantic Markup: One of the main critiques I received was related to the lack of proper semantic markups on my website. It became evident that I hadn't utilized HTML tags effectively to convey the meaning and structure of my content. By not using tags like header, nav, section, and footer appropriately, I missed out on providing clear cues to search engines and assistive technologies.
Headers and Microformats: Another area that required improvement was the implementation of headers and microformats. The feedback pointed out that I hadn't structured my content using hierarchical headers (H1, H2, etc.) effectively. This led to a lack of readability and clarity for both users and search engine crawlers. Additionally, I overlooked the significance of microformats, which provide additional context to certain elements like addresses, events, or reviews. Their absence hindered the website's overall accessibility and search engine optimization (SEO).
Image Performance: The feedback further highlighted the need to enhance the performance of the images on my website. Images played a significant role in my content, but their sizes were inconsistent, resulting in slower loading times. This negatively affected user experience and could potentially discourage visitors from staying on my website.
Receiving constructive feedback about my website's performance was a valuable experience that helped me recognize the areas where I fell short. The importance of using proper semantic markups, headers, and microformats cannot be overstated, as they greatly impact a website's accessibility, search engine visibility, and overall user experience. Additionally, optimizing image performance is crucial for faster load times and a seamless browsing experience. Armed with this knowledge, I'm now motivated to revisit my website and make the necessary improvements, ensuring that my future online endeavors will be better equipped to meet the expectations of both users and search engines alike.
